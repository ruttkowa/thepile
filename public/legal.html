Privacy policy

Datenschutzerklärung · GDPR

1. Overview

The Pile is a static, client-side web app with no backend server of its own. It does not use cookies, analytics, advertising, or any form of visitor tracking, and it has no user accounts. This policy explains the very limited data processing that still happens as a technical consequence of hosting a website and calling the Scryfall API.

Like any web host, GitHub's servers automatically process technical access data (e.g. IP address, browser type, date and time of the request) to deliver the page and for their own security and operational purposes. The operator of The Pile has no access to and does not receive this data. See GitHub's Privacy Statement for details.

4. Data stored in your browser

Your filters, piles, and swipe sessions are stored exclusively in your own browser's local storage. This data is never transmitted to the operator of The Pile or to any server the operator controls. It stays on your device until you clear it yourself (via the app's own delete/export controls, or your browser's site data settings), and is never shared, sold, or analyzed.

5. Scryfall API (third-party service)

Card search, card data, and card images are requested directly by your browser from Scryfall's servers (api.scryfall.com, cards.scryfall.io) — not proxied through any server operated by The Pile. Scryfall may process your IP address and request metadata as part of ordinary request handling on their end. This processing is outside the operator's control; see Scryfall's Privacy Policy.

6. No cookies, no analytics, no advertising

The Pile does not set cookies, does not use any analytics or tracking service (no Google Analytics, no Matomo, no pixel trackers), and displays no advertising.

7. Your rights under the GDPR

Because so little personal data is processed by the operator directly, most of these rights will have nothing to act on — but for completeness, under Articles 15–21 GDPR you have the right to request access to, rectification or erasure of, and restriction of processing of your personal data, the right to data portability, and the right to object to processing. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority (Art. 77 GDPR), in particular in the German state where the operator resides.
